THE HISTORY OF NU METAL

HONORABLE MENTIONS

Beyond the biggest names in the genre, many other bands made significant contributions to the nu metal movement. These artists helped shape the sound and aesthetic of the era, each bringing their unique elements to the genre's diverse soundscape.

LIMP BIZKIT

Known for their aggressive sound and frontman Fred Durst's confrontational style, they became one of nu metal's most commercially successful acts with hits like "Nookie" and "Break Stuff".

LINKIN PARK

Combining rap, rock, and electronic elements, they became one of the best-selling bands of the 21st century with their debut album "Hybrid Theory" defining the nu metal sound for a generation.

SLIPKNOT

With their distinctive masks and jumpsuits, nine-member lineup, and chaotic, percussive sound, they pushed nu metal to heavier, more extreme territories.

SYSTEM OF A DOWN

Blending nu metal with avant-garde elements, progressive rock, and Armenian folk music, they created a unique sound and addressed political themes in their lyrics.

GENRE INFLUENCE

While often criticized by traditional metal fans, nu metal brought heavy music to new audiences and expanded the possibilities of what metal could be. Its influence can still be heard today in various genres from trap metal to modern rock.
