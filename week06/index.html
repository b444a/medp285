THE Y2K FAD

Nu metal hits you like a punch to the gut – raw, loud, and impossible to ignore. Bands like Korn, Limp Bizkit, and Linkin Park took heavy metal and threw in hip-hop beats, scratching turntables, and aggressive rap vocals to create something entirely new. Those down-tuned guitars and primal screams captured exactly what it felt like to be young, angry, and misunderstood in a world that didn't make sense. The mosh pits were wild, the lyrics were honest, and the sound was heavy enough to make your parents worry. Nu metal wasn't just music – it was the soundtrack for a generation looking for something real in a fake world. While critics turned up their noses, fans found a community where they could let out all their pent-up frustration and actually belong. Even today, that unmistakable nu metal sound still hits as hard as it did when it first crashed onto the scene.
